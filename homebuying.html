7 STEPS TO BECOME A HOMEWONER

VIEW JOURNEY

Step 1

Find an Agent

  • Interview agents to find someone who fits you and your needs
  • Commit to working with one real estate professional
  • Discuss with your agent your home search wants and needs
Read more

Finding a competent and experienced buyer’s agent with local expertise will start you on the right path of your homebuying journey. A buyer’s agent is there to guide you through the home buying process, protect your money, and negotiate for you while keeping the process as stress-free as possible, so be sure you feel comfortable with them. They have access to resources(lenders, inspectors, search programs, and more) and experience with the process, so you can focus on finding your perfecthome, and not on learning real estate sales. And, the services of a buyer’s agent are paid for by the seller, not you, so use them!

Step 2

Mortgage Pre-approval

  • Interview lenders to find someone who fits you and your needs
  • CLearn your true purchasing power by getting a full pre-approval
  • Review the costs involved in buying, and begin budgeting and saving for your purchase
Read more

Getting a great lender can save you money and make your offers a lot more competitive. They will also help you understand and plan for everything finance related: credit score, down payments, closings costs, interest rates and more. Be sure you find someone who explains all of your expected costs to you. They should tell you what your monthly payments will look like along with what your closing costs should be.

Step 3

House Hunting

  • Now starts the fun! Use the Kendrick property search tools to find great homes
  • Attend private home tours of properties scheduled by your agent
  • Keep notes and running list of likes, dislikes, and questions about properties
  • Update your agent on any changes in your wants and needs
Read more

Now starts the fun part! Search online and with your agent’s help to find new homes. Your agent will coordinate private home tours on your schedule, and get you facts and details about the home that don’t show up on the internet. Be sure when you are searching for homes to keep notes, and continually update your list of “must-have” and “nice-to-have”, to help narrow down your search. Your agent should also be sending you properties as soon as, or even before they hit the market, so you stay ahead of the competition.

Step 4

Offers and Negotiation

  • You found a home you love – now it's time to write an offer!
  • Review market trends to decide on an offer price and terms of the offer
  • Work with your agent to write and sign your purchase contract
  • Send purchase contract and other necessary documents to the seller
  • If the seller counters your offer, with the help of your agent you can negotiate on final terms
Read more

When you find the home for you, it’s time to decide on offer terms. Price is a key part, as are other components such as financing terms, concessions or requests of the seller, and timelines for the purchase. Your agent will perform a market analysis to determine the best offer price, help put together your best offer, and advocate for you to the seller. They will also negotiate with the sellers, in the event of a counter-offer. A good agent knows how to look at the whole picture use the different components of an offer, not just purchase price, to get your offer accepted on the most favorable terms.

Step 5

Home Inspections

  • Schedule inspectors to give you a detailed report on the condition of the property
  • It is typically best to request a home, roof, and pest inspection
  • Negotiate with the seller on any repairs to be done or credits to be given
Read more

This is your chance to kick the tires on the home and make sure you aren’t falling into a money pit. Choosing a good inspector is important, as they will identify any issues with the home, and help to understand their severity. You are also free to negotiate again with the seller to revise the terms of the offer, if you are not satisfied with the condition of the home. Here your agent is involved every step of the way, recommending and scheduling inspections, negotiating, and ensuring you stay within your contract contingencies.

Final Step

Closing

  • Schedule and sign all loan and title documents
  • Record the transfer of title at the county recorder’s office
  • Get your keys and move in!
Read more

This is when you get the keys to your new home! Well first, there is the signing of loan documents, title documents, and finally recording the sale with the county. At closing, be prepared to pay closing costs, such as third-party fees for the transaction. These vary greatly city to city and even sale to sale, and often include lender fees, title and escrow fees, county and city fees, and more. Your lender should discuss closing costs long before the close of escrow. If not, ask your agent for more information.
